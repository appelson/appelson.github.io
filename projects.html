Washington et al. V. Smith et al.

The lawsuit data analysis aims to demonstrate that the St. Tammany Parish Sheriffâ€™s Office discriminates against African Americans through their traffic stops. This research includes a large-scale predictive analysis of the demographics of those stopped, their citations, and an OCR of citation PDFs. From January through November 2023, we found that Black individuals in St. Tammany Parish were 250% more likely to be stopped for alleged traffic violations than white individuals. Although 15% of the parish's residents identify as Black, they account for 36% of those stopped and 26% of those cited. This lawsuit is ongoing.

The Advocate Media Analysis

The Advocate analysis examines how media coverage influences perceptions of crime in Louisiana. We analyzed every article from The Advocate from 2016 and found that out of 102,029 news articles, 27,799 (27%) were in the "police and crime" category. Coverage varied from 34% in 2016 to 18% in 2023, with fluctuations in between, including an increase to 24% in 2021.

One vs. Two-year Statute of Limitation Impact Factsheet

The Statute of Limitation Impact Factsheet challenges the claim that shorter statutes of limitations lead to more meritorious cases. Our analysis suggests that extending the statute of limitations for police action cases in Louisiana could reduce court caseloads and result in more meritorious cases. Additionally, we found that shorter statutes disproportionately impact Black communities.
